<!-- Three Pillars Section -->
<section class="section section-pillars">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <span class="section-label">What You Get</span>
      <h2>Exchange ambiguity for clarity, then turn clarity into repeatable execution</h2>
      <p class="section-desc">Agent-first workflows only help if they're governable and repeatable—otherwise you just automate chaos.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pillars-grid">
      <div class="pillar-card">
        <div class="pillar-number">01</div>
        <h3>Onboard with clarity from the source of truth</h3>
        <p>Query multi-repo codebases and docs to build accurate mental models fast—architecture, ownership boundaries, dependencies, and "what's safe to change."</p>
        <ul class="pillar-benefits">
          <li>Fewer "where is this implemented?" loops</li>
          <li>Faster first PRs with less risk</li>
          <li>Shared understanding, not tribal knowledge</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="pillar-card">
        <div class="pillar-number">02</div>
        <h3>Standardize delivery across people and projects</h3>
        <p>Turn your delivery process into deterministic workflows: explicit steps, constrained tools, validation, and quality gates—so outcomes don't depend on who's assigned.</p>
        <ul class="pillar-benefits">
          <li>Consistent PR quality regardless of who's assigned</li>
          <li>Reusable workflow templates across clients</li>
          <li>Proof you can show: what ran, what validated</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="pillar-card">
        <div class="pillar-number">03</div>
        <h3>Earn client trust by design</h3>
        <p>Fully on-prem, any LLM, OpenTelemetry instrumentation, and open-source core—auditable, inspectable, and compatible with enterprise constraints.</p>
        <ul class="pillar-benefits">
          <li>Easier security conversations</li>
          <li>Clearer accountability</li>
          <li>Less vendor anxiety</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

<!-- FAQ Section -->
<section class="section section-faq">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="section-header">
      <span class="section-label">FAQ</span>
      <h2>Common Questions from Agencies</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="faq-grid">
      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Can we run this entirely inside a client's environment?</h3>
        <p>Yes—this is designed to run on-prem. You can keep code, logs, and artifacts inside the client boundary. No data ever leaves their infrastructure.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Are we locked into one model provider?</h3>
        <p>No—model choice is yours (and often dictated by the client). Workflows are model-agnostic by design. Use Claude, GPT, open-source, or self-hosted models.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>How do we keep quality consistent across engineers and timezones?</h3>
        <p>By codifying standards as pipelines: explicit steps, constrained tools, schema validation, checks, and observable runs. The workflow behaves the same regardless of who runs it.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Can each client have different workflows?</h3>
        <p>Yes. You can reuse a base workflow pack and customize per client/repo/team, with changes versioned and reviewed like any code.</p>
      </div>
